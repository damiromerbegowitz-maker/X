For Daria — The Path Through The Quiet Woods

The Fog

“I don’t know if I’m enough to walk this path.”

The guide smiled: “You do not need to be ‘enough’ before you begin. You only need to begin.”

The Weight

“Why does my heart feel so heavy?”

The guide said: “You are carrying every doubt, every regret, every ‘what if’. Set them down for a moment. The road is easier when you only carry what is yours to carry today.”

The River

“What if I fall?”

“Whether you step forward or stay still,” the guide replied, “time will pass. So step, and let the river teach you balance.”

The Mirror Leaf

“I see nothing good in me,” said the traveler.

“The leaf does not need to see the sun to hold its light,” said the guide. “You too reflect more goodness than you know.”

The Mountain

“It’s too far. I will never reach it.”

“The mountain does not care if you arrive today or in ten years,” the guide said. “Each step is a victory over standing still.”

The Bench

“I feel guilty resting.”

“Rest is not the enemy of progress,” the guide answered. “Even the strongest trees grow in stillness.”

The Sunrise

The traveler stood taller, breathing deeply.

“You are not behind. You are not less. You are exactly where you need to be for the next step.”

For Daria, whose light is far greater than she sees
