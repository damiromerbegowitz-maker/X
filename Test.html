The Starling's Flight

The Starling felt small in a vast forest, her wings heavy with doubt, as if the sky were too grand for her. The Willow stood nearby, its branches swaying gently, whispering, “Your wings are enough, even when they feel frail.”

The Starling tried to fly but stumbled, her heart whispering she’d never soar. The Willow’s roots held the earth steady, its voice soft, “Falling is part of flight. Each stumble carves your path.”

A storm darkened the sky, and the Starling hid, trembling. The Willow bent in the wind but did not break, murmuring, “The storm shapes you, but it is not you. You are the light beyond it.”

The Starling watched other birds soar, their wings bold, and felt unworthy. The Willow pointed to her blue feathers, gleaming faintly, and said, “No other bird carries your light.”

Exhausted, the Starling rested on the Willow’s branches, her heart heavy. The Willow offered shade, its voice steady, “Rest is not surrender; it’s gathering strength for the skies ahead.”

The Starling feared her song was too faint, too flawed. The Willow hummed softly, its branches swaying, “Your voice matters, even if it trembles. The world hears it.”

The Starling flew higher, hesitantly, her wings unsteady. The Willow swayed below, its voice clear, “Courage is not the absence of fear, but moving through it, one beat at a time.”

The Starling saw a distant star, its glow mirrored in her feathers. The Willow nodded, its branches still, “You’ve always carried the light you seek. It was never out of reach.”

The Starling soared, her wings wide, the sky no longer vast but hers. The Willow stood below, silent, its presence a quiet strength, watching her claim the stars.

For Daria, whose light shines even in the quiet moments, a starling who will always find her sky.
