The Butterfly and the Oak
In the quiet forest, the Butterfly fluttered low, her wings heavy with the weight of unseen storms. "I am but a fleeting shadow," she whispered to the wind, "unworthy of the sky's vast embrace."
The Oak, rooted deep in the earth, extended a branch. "Storms pass," he murmured, "but they do not define the strength of your wings. What you endure shapes the grace with which you rise."
"Why must I fight the winds that others glide upon?" the Butterfly asked, her colors fading in the doubt. The Oak replied, "The winds are impartial teachers. Embrace what they reveal about your unyielding core."
In the rain, she sought shelter beneath his leaves. "You stand tall without question," she said. "I have faced centuries of tempests," the Oak answered, "yet I grow not in spite of them, but because of the quiet resolve they demand."
"Am I deserving of the sun's warmth?" the Butterfly pondered aloud. The Oak's branches swayed gently. "Worth is not earned in perfection, but recognized in the simple act of being. The sun shines on all, without judgment."
As the Butterfly ascended, she turned back. "Your roots hold you firm." The Oak rustled, "And your wings carry you far. In our differences, we find the balance that endures."
For Daria, whose quiet strength lights the forest, even on the stormiest days.
